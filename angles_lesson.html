Angles and Turning

Drag things. Notice what changes.

Two arms, joined at a point.

Drag the gold dot.

The opening between the two arms is called an angle. A small opening, a wide opening, a wraps-most-of-the-way-around opening — all angles.

Putting a number on the opening.

One way to measure an angle: imagine the moving arm starts on top of the fixed arm and then turns. A whole way around — one full turn back to where it started — we call 360°. (The little circle just means “this number is an angle.”)

Drag the dot. Watch the number.

opening: 60°

Drag the arm to make a quarter of a full turn. What number do you see?

Then drag to check.

Two square corners stacked together (an “L” plus another “L”) make what angle?

A square corner is a quarter turn.

A clock face.

Two hands joined at the middle of a clock. The short hand is the hour hand; the long hand is the minute hand. Drag either one.

How many hour-marks go around the clock face?

A whole way around is 360°, and the hour-marks split that turn into equal pieces. How many degrees from one hour-mark to the very next one?

Set the long hand on 12 and the short hand on 3. What is the angle between them?
