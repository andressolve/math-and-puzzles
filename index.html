Math and Puzzles

A small collection of weekend tests, practice sets, and lessons.

Interactive lessons

  • Angles and Turning interactive

    Drag two arms and a pair of clock hands. From “what is an angle?” all the way to reading the dial. No telling, just doing.

Saturday tests

  • Saturday Test No. 3 PDF

    Eight problems, fifteen minutes. Numbers and Shapes · Counting Carefully · Reasoning.
  • Saturday Test No. 4 PDF

    Eight problems, fifteen minutes. Variables, clock angles, lattice paths, Venn, knights & knaves, water jugs.
  • Saturday Test No. 5 PDF

    Eight problems, fifteen minutes. Place value, perfect squares, the digit-sum test for divisibility by 3, area of a parallelogram, postage stamps, the sock-drawer puzzle, and trailing zeros of a factorial.
  • Saturday Test No. 6 PDF

    Eight problems, fifteen minutes. A doubling thread — paper folds, halving a square, a water lily, the ×2+1 pattern, a spreading secret, a knockout tournament — then a Find the Angle finish with crossing lines and a parallelogram built from four lines.
  • Saturday Test No. 7 PDF

    Eight problems, fifteen minutes. An odd-and-even thread — the discovery that parity quietly settles a puzzle without trying every case. Sums and splits, then flipping cups, a grasshopper's jumps, a class pairing up, and hats around a round table.

Practice sets

  • Practice Set No. 1 — Coins and Weighings PDF

    Six untimed problems, scaffolded from three outcomes up to nine coins in two weighings.
  • Practice Set No. 2 — Counting with Overlaps PDF

    Six untimed problems building the inclusion–exclusion idea: see the double-count, then subtract it.
  • Practice Set No. 3 — Dominoes and the Checkerboard PDF

    Six untimed problems. Start by covering a board with paper dominoes, then discover why a chessboard with two opposite corners removed can never be tiled.

Tools and paper

  • Quarter-inch grid paper PDF

    One page of light grid for working out problems by hand.
  • Domino cut-out sheet PDF

    Paper dominoes and a small board to cut out — the hands-on piece for Practice Set No. 3, problem 1.
